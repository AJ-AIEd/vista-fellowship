Context

Why This, Why Now

You've probably already noticed it: a student turns in something polished, and you genuinely can't tell whether they understood it or AI carried them.

That's not a discipline problem on your end. It's that most assignments were built for a world where "looks good" reliably meant "thought hard." That's not true anymore.

This isn't about catching AI use. It's about redesigning the task so you can see the thinking again — whether or not AI was involved.

August launch session

You'll Do This With Me First

At the August launch, you'll watch me audit one of my own real tasks live — including the parts where I'm not sure what to put. Then you'll try it yourself, with a partner, on a different real task of mine where I already know what changed and why.

By the end of that one session, you'll have done this once before you ever do it alone.

Bring a task you're genuinely unsure about for after that session — that's the one you'll redesign for Cycle 1.
The question behind all of this
If AI can produce the answer, how do you grade the thinking behind it?

That's it. Every part of this fellowship — the audit, the thinking routine, the reflection at the end — is just structured ways of answering that one question for one task at a time.
